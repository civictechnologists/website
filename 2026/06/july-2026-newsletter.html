ACT Newsletter July 2026: Director’s Update, CityCamp, ACT Congress 2026

June 29, 2026

Director’s Update

As we celebrate Independence Day, we want to thank people who are committed to the ideals of democracy and to making their neighborhoods better places for everyone. So, thank you.

The ACT board and our staff have been busy getting our house in order. I’m excited to say that we are now an official 501(c)3! In the next few weeks, we’ll be updating our donation page and website to reflect our new status. We want to thank Jake for all his hard work in making this happen.

We’re also excited to announce that we’ll be having our first in-person conference this fall in Orlando, Florida with the help of Florida Community Innovation! Read down below for more info!

Finally, it’s time for our annual membership renewal. Delegates should watch their inboxes for more! Once again, thank you for all you do.

CityCamp

Save the date for World CityCamp Day on September 19, 2026! CityCamp is a community-driven unconference that brings together residents, government staff, civic leaders, technologists, designers, entrepreneurs, and journalists to collaborate on local challenges and opportunities. Since it’s beginnings in Chicago in 2010, CityCamp events have empowered communities worldwide to explore topics ranging from civic engagement and open government to housing, transportation, education, and public health. Nicole Dan from FCI is helping coordinate this year’s efforts, and we encourage everyone interested in strengthening their community through collaboration and innovation to get involved. Please email nicole.dan@floridainnovation.org to receive updates on weekly CityCamp planning meetings. Watch future ACT newsletters for updates on CityCamp Day activities and ways to participate.

Congress

Mark your calendars for ACT Congress 2026! Hosted this year by Florida Community Innovation (FCI) in Orlando, this annual gathering brings together civic technologists, community advocates, nonprofit leaders, and volunteers from across the country to connect, collaborate, and build technology for social good. With unconference sessions, hands-on volunteering opportunities, and skill-building workshops led by ACT leaders. This will be a unique moment to learn, share ideas, and strengthen the civic tech community. Nicole Dan and Erin Enabnit from FCI are helping coordinate this year’s event. Dates and registration details are still being finalized, and there will be a cost to attend. Stay tuned to the ACT Newsletter for updates and information on how to get involved.

Stay in Touch

Interested in chatting with the community in real time, join our public Discord server!

Does your local civic tech organization have a story to tell? Want to be featured in our next newsletter? Reach out to us at hello@civictechnologists.org.

The Alliance of Civic Technologists (ACT) is an organization that connects volunteer civic technology efforts in both technology and advocacy within local communities. Formed in 2023, ACT's goal is to ensure the continuity and growth of civic technology volunteer communities across the United States.
