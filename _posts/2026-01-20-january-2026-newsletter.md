### Director’s Update: One Year In

On January 20th, we wrote a [joint blog post](https://community.publicgood.tech/c/announcements/one-year-in) with [Technologists for Public Good](https://www.publicgood.tech) and [We the Builders](https://www.wethebuilders.org) highlighting the impact of the Trump administration’s first year on public interest technology. The numbers that we’re looking at are striking, as hundreds of digital service experts were kicked out of government alongside nearly 300,000 federal employees. The United State Digital Service was transformed into the United States Department of Government Efficiency Service (DOGE). DOGE was then subsequently responsible for not only dissolving civic tech teams across government, but the dissolution of entire departments and agencies such as USAID. 18F was dissolved after 10 years of helping governments purchase, build and improve their technology through a focus on human centered design, agile and open source practices.

It’s been rough, but now is not the time for doomerism. We’ve seen an uptick in states and cities expanding their digital service roles and we saw an uptick in volunteer civic engagement across the board. We’re finding that civic technology volunteering these days looks less like building an app and looks more like grunt work. This could be helping a street level non-profit with their spreadsheets or teaching immigration attorneys the basics of cybersecurity.

For 2026, the three priorities that our three organizations identified were investing in the people behind the work, fighting the unethical uses of technology, and preparing to rebuild.  The fundamental question facing civic tech in 2026 isn't technical—it's political and moral: Who should technology serve?

I hope you’ll [join us](https://www.civictechnologists.org/join) in helping to answer that question.

### Network Roundup

Here are some quick notes about what’s been happening in the network!

#### Florida Community Innovation Seeks Coordinator

Do you love using your organizational and project management skills for good? [Florida Community Innovation Foundation](https://floridainnovation.org/) could use your help! Specifically, their executive director, [Caroline Nickerson](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=fGIOuPP8jYE), PhD could use support coordinating with both community partners and student teams to keep civic tech projects on track. Some of these projects include: FCI's public affairs efforts, the [Florida Resource Map](https://www.floridaresourcemap.org/), and more. Please email [info@floridainnovation.org](mailto:info@floridainnovation.org) if you're interested!

#### Open Source San José Supports Mapping Initiatives

Every other week, [Open Source San José](https://opensourcesanjose.org/) partners with [Bay Area OpenStreetMappers](https://www.meetup.com/bay-area-openstreetmappers/) to host [South Bay Map Night](https://www.meetup.com/bay-area-openstreetmappers/events/312977806/), a welcoming gathering of [OpenStreetMap](https://www.openstreetmap.org/) enthusiasts from across San José and the South Bay. Participants share tagging tips, plan new mapping projects, and connect around all things OpenStreetMap, including occasional explorations of [OpenHistoricalMap](https://www.openhistoricalmap.org/). Interested parties are encouraged to join no matter the skill level, with more information available on the [South Bay OSM homepage](https://github.com/codeforsanjose/OSM-SouthBay/).

#### Chi Hack Night Hosted Civic Tech Leaders

A recent panel discussion, hosted by [Chi Hack Night](https://chihacknight.org/), brought together [Reilly Martin](https://www.linkedin.com/in/reilly-martin/) (Technologists for the Public Good), [Kate Green](https://www.linkedin.com/in/4kategreen/) (We the Builders), and [Christopher Whitaker](https://www.linkedin.com/in/christopherwhitaker/) (Alliance of Civic Technologists) to reflect on how recent policy changes, personnel shifts, and evolving uses of technology are shaping the civic tech landscape. The conversation centered on transparency, human-centered government, and the ethical responsibilities facing technologists today. Watch the full discussion, now available to watch on [YouTube](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=QSkiVi9DXPA).
